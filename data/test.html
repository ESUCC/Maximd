Interpretations--IQ and Index Scores

The WISC-IV yields four index scores, as well as a full scale IQ. Each of the indexes is derived from a combination of selected subtests.

The Verbal Comprehension Index (VCI) is a measure of verbal knowledge and comprehension, reflecting proficiency in language and culture. The VCI is often seen as a good predictor of scholastic achievement. Bailees VCI score of 93 placed her verbal ability at the average level.

The Perceptual Reasoning Index (PRI) provides a measure of fluid reasoning and perceptual and organizational skills. The PRI reflects cultural experience and school-based knowledge to a much lesser extent than the VCI. Bailee obtained a PRI score of 98, which is average.

The Working Memory Index (WMI) is a measure of short-term auditory memory, concentration, and attention. Bailees score on the WMI is 91, representing average ability in this domain.

The Processing Speed Index (PSI) is a measure of timed activity involving the processing of nonverbal visual information. Bailee received a score of 88 on this index, indicating that she demonstrates low average processing speed ability.

The Full Scale IQ (FSIQ) comprises all 10 core subtests and is a measure of overall intellectual functioning. Bailees FSIQ is 90, indicating average overall ability. There are no significant differences among the index scores, suggesting that Bailee possesses relatively comparable abilities across the assessed domains.

Verbal Comprehension Subtests

The Similarities (SI) subtest measures abstract and concrete verbal reasoning abilities. Bailees score of 10 on SI is average. She can usually recognize how objects and concepts are alike and what commonalities are present.

The Vocabulary (VC) subtest measures word fluency, ability to understand and use words, and level of verbal experience. Bailees VC score is 8, indicating her ability in this area is average. She is able to use words and understand their meaning.

Bailees score of 8 on the Comprehension (CO) subtest is average, indicating an expected level of common sense, social knowledge, and judgment of practical situations.

Perceptual Reasoning Subtests

The Block Design (BD) subtest measures nonverbal concept formation, perceptual skills, and visual-motor integration. Bailee performed in the average range on this subtest, with a score of 9, indicating that she possesses typical skills in nonverbal reasoning and working well under time pressure.

Bailee obtained a score of 7 on the Picture Concepts (PCn) subtest, which is low average compared to her same-age peers. In addition to assessing categorical and abstract reasoning, this subtest also measures an individuals ability to use verbal mediation to assist with a visual task. Her performance suggests below average reasoning and visual perceptual capabilities.

The Matrix Reasoning (MR) subtest measures visual processing and reasoning, as well as concentration and attention to detail. This student obtained a score of 13, which is high average. This score indicates that Bailee can process visual stimuli and has good nonverbal reasoning skills.

Working Memory Subtests

Bailee obtained a Digit Span (DS) score of 7, which is low average. This score indicates some difficulties in short-term verbal memory, concentration, verbal reorganization ability, and attention span.

The Letter-Number Sequencing (LN) subtest is a measure of attention span, short-term auditory working memory, and sequencing abilities. Bailee obtained a score of 10 on this subtest, which is in the average range and indicates that she has an expected level of skill in this area.

Processing Speed Subtests

The Coding (CD) subtest measures short-term nonverbal memory, short-term learning of nonverbal symbols, visual perception, and fine-motor coordination. Her score of 7 is low average and suggests that she shows some weakness in these skills under time pressure.

The Symbol Search (SS) subtest measures the students ability to note detail quickly and make rapid decisions, as well as her perceptual and visual-motor abilities. Bailees score of 9 on SS is average, suggesting fair skills in quick and accurate perception.
